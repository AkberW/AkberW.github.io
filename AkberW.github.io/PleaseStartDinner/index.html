ABOUT US

One day, on my way back from work, my husband calls and says, "I'm home early! I've started to prepare dinner!" I'm thrilled and walk in the door with a lift in my step. I wash up, and start to help. He "downs tools" and opens the fridge, looking for a beverage. I start to shout: "Why have you stopped helping? Why do I have to be the one to lead the charge all the time?" Turns out he was looking to find my favourite beverage, to pour one for me, welcoming me home.

You can well image the rest of this unpleasant exchange. All this unnecessary drama, when we had good intentions of preparing a family dinner and being kind to one another. I had to change things, if I wanted to stay married. I asked a friend's son who is a culinary student to come in one afternoon to cook. I'm happy to report that everyone is now speaking to each other, and on a full stomach.


Our family was having trouble fitting in the stuff we need to do between 6 and 8pm after school and work - let alone fitting in the things we wanted to do:


  • Pick up kid(s)
  • Pick up some emergency groceries - like milk and a pizza/rotisserie chicken/sushi
  • Prep and cook dinner
  • Talk about the day and share parental wisdom to solve kid's conundrum
  • Help with homework
  • Prepare lunches for tomorrow
  • Prepare clothes and school bags
  • Read together as a family
  • Brush hair (this is separate for a good reason, trust me)
  • Get them in the bath (this is supposed to be relaxing?)
  • Have some cuddle time and time for the bedtime story
  • Clean up after dinner, and resist that extra glass of wine or cookie that just might make it better

I'm sure your family has a few more to add. We find that giving up nightly cooking cost us a little in terms of the brightness of the broccoli, but we gained more time with each other.
